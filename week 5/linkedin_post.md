# 📈 Optimizing Direct Marketing Campaigns: A Business Analytics Case Study 📊

How can retail banks increase term deposit subscriptions while reducing call center costs? 🤷‍♂️

As part of the **AnalystLab Africa Data Analytics Internship Program** (Week 5 Case Study), I conducted a deep dive into the **Bank Marketing Dataset** (11,162 customer contacts) to identify what drives campaign conversion and how to eliminate waste.

Here are the key takeaways from my analysis:

### 🔍 Key Insights Discovered
1️⃣ **The Call Duration Effect ⏱️**: Call duration is the #1 predictor of conversion. Subscription rate jumps from **9%** (calls under 2 min) to **85%** (calls over 9.5 min). Keeping customers engaged is vital!
2️⃣ **May Campaign Fatigue 📅**: The bank placed its highest volume of calls in May (2,824 calls), yet May yielded the lowest conversion rate (**32.7%**). Meanwhile, off-peak months like March and December recorded conversion rates over **90%** with a fraction of the call volume.
3️⃣ **Diminishing Returns of Spasmodic Calls 📞**: Calling a customer multiple times is counterproductive. The first attempt has a **53%** conversion rate, but it drops to **31%** by the 6th call and **22%** after 10+ calls.
4️⃣ **High-Conversion Demographics 👥**: Students (**74.7%**) and Retirees (**66.3%**) are highly receptive, as are cellular-contacted clients (**54.3%**) and those with higher balances (**58.0%**).

---

### 💡 My Actionable Recommendations
🔹 **Reallocate Budgets**: Shift agent capacity away from the over-saturated May campaigns into high-converting transitional months (March, September, October, December).
🔹 **Call Capping**: Cap campaign contact frequency at **3 attempts** per customer to save agent hours and protect customer experience.
🔹 **Targeted Offers**: Design tailored, steady-yield savings plans specifically for retirees and flexible micro-savings plans for students.
🔹 **Data Enrichment**: Eliminate "unknown" contact methods (currently converting at only 22.5%) by prioritizing cellular data collection.
